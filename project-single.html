Project Overview

The Disability Rights Advocacy Initiative is a comprehensive campaign led by NDWA to advance inclusive policies and accessibility rights for women and girls with disabilities across all seven provinces of Nepal. This multi-year project combines evidence-based advocacy, community mobilization, and strategic partnerships to create systemic change.

Project Goals

  • Advocate for legal and policy reforms that protect and promote the rights of women with disabilities
  • Build capacity of women with disabilities to lead advocacy efforts
  • Strengthen coordination among government, CSOs, and women with disabilities
  • Ensure women with disabilities participate in all decision-making processes
  • Promote accessibility in all sectors (health, education, employment, services)

Key Activities

Policy Advocacy

Engaging with government at local, provincial, and national levels to ensure laws and policies reflect the needs and priorities of women with disabilities. NDWA conducts research, submits policy briefs, and participates in consultations.

Community Mobilization

Organizing women with disabilities and allies to participate in advocacy campaigns, protests, and community dialogue on disability rights issues.

Capacity Building

Providing training to women with disabilities on advocacy skills, rights education, and leadership development to strengthen their ability to advocate for themselves.

Strategic Partnerships

Collaborating with other CSOs, government agencies, international organizations, and donors to amplify advocacy messages and ensure coordinated action.

Expected Outcomes

  • At least 5 new laws or policies passed reflecting disability rights and inclusion
  • 1,000+ women with disabilities trained in advocacy and leadership
  • Women with disabilities represented in 50+ government planning and decision-making bodies
  • Improved accessibility in key public services and infrastructure
  • Strengthened networks and coordination among disability rights organizations

Project Budget & Funding

Total Project Budget: USD 800,000

Funded by development partners and donors committed to advancing disability rights and gender equality in Nepal.

Progress to Date

Since the project launch in 2023, NDWA has made significant progress:

  • Successfully advocated for inclusion of disability provisions in 3 provincial laws
  • Trained 450+ women with disabilities in advocacy and rights
  • Established women's disability networks in 5 provinces
  • Conducted 20+ community awareness campaigns
  • Engaged with 15+ government agencies on disability issues
